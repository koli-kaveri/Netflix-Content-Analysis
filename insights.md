# 📊 Netflix Content Strategy — Business Insights Report
**Dataset:** netflix_movies_detailed_up_to_2025.csv | 16,000 Movies | 2010–2025

---

## 🔑 Key Findings

### 1. Drama Dominates — Invest More, Not Less
Drama leads all genres with **6,910 titles** — 2.8× more than Comedy. 
It also achieves the highest average audience engagement. Netflix should increase Drama 
original production by **20% YoY** and prioritize international Drama co-productions.

### 2. Thriller Is the Fastest-Growing Genre
Thriller grew **400%+** from 2015 to 2022 — faster than any other major genre. 
Psychological thrillers show strong ROI given relatively moderate budgets. 
**Recommendation:** Greenlight 15–20 Thriller originals per year.

### 3. Korean & Japanese Content Is Critically Underinvested
- Korea: 875 titles (5.5% of library) — but K-Drama has proven global viral potential
- Japan: 990 titles — drives anime demand worldwide
- **Gap:** Neither market has enough exclusive Netflix originals
- **Action:** Double exclusive content deals with Korean and Japanese studios within 2 years

### 4. India Content Gap = Biggest Missed Opportunity
India contributes only **528 titles** despite:
- 1.4 billion population (largest potential subscriber base)
- Fastest-growing smartphone and broadband market
- Bollywood being the world's most prolific film industry
- **Action:** Triple Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and Bengali original content investment

### 5. France Is Quietly Outperforming
France is the **#3 content-producing country** (1,701 titles) with strong European audience 
retention. French thrillers and dramas consistently rate well.
**Action:** Aggressively market French originals in non-Francophone markets (Latin America, Africa).

### 6. Family & Animation Are Underfunded vs. Retention Value
Family (1,472) and Animation (1,579) are lower-volume genres but:
- Serve household accounts → **higher subscription retention**
- Drive multi-user account value
- **Action:** Increase animated originals by 30% to reduce household churn

### 7. Average Rating Is 5.96/10 — Quality Bar Needs Raising
Only **~26% of movies** score 7.0 or above. The ML model (AUC: 0.82) can predict 
high-rated movies before acquisition using engagement signals and genre/language features.
**Action:** Implement ML-based quality scoring as a pre-acquisition filter.

### 8. Q4 Is Peak Season — Deploy Best Content Then
The heatmap shows **Oct–Dec** sees the highest content additions every year.
- Q4 aligns with holiday viewing, awards season, and new subscription signups
- **Action:** Schedule highest-budget releases Oct–Dec; use Jan–Mar for mid-tier content

### 9. Vote Count Is the #1 Success Predictor
The Random Forest model identified `vote_count` (audience engagement) as the 
**most important feature** for predicting high-rated movies — more than genre, language, or budget.
**Action:** Track early engagement signals; double marketing spend on films showing high 
engagement in the first 2 weeks of release.

### 10. Horror = High Volume, Low Cost, Young Audience
Horror (2,425 titles) delivers maximum titles-per-dollar:
- Lower average production budgets than Action/Adventure
- Attracts Gen-Z subscribers (18–24 demographic)
- **Action:** Maintain Horror volume; prioritize original horror franchises over one-offs
